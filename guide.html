Trip Operations Console

User Guide & Feature Set

One screen to plan and run the whole trip — itinerary, calendar, to-dos, notes, spending, and points — on your phone or laptop, with nothing to install.

The console keeps every moving part of the trip in one place so you are not jumping between a spreadsheet, a notes app, and a calendar. Everything you type is saved automatically as you go, and the whole thing works on a phone, a tablet, or a computer in any modern browser.

Getting started

  • Open the link you were sent in any browser. On a phone, use your browser's Add to Home Screen for one-tap access; on a computer, bookmark it.
  • Just start typing — there is no save button. Changes are kept the moment you make them.
  • The search bar at the top searches across everything at once — itinerary, expenses, to-dos, notes, and more. Start typing and jump straight to a match.
  • Use the ☀ / 🌙 toggle (top right) to switch between light and dark.
  • Low Data Mode (the ⚡ control) strips the app down for slow or expensive connections — handy when you are travelling abroad. It now switches on automatically when it detects a slow connection.

Itinerary

The backbone of the trip, laid out day by day.

  • Give any item a time, a duration, and a category — and add, edit, or remove items freely.
  • Each day carries its own destination timezone (Cairo, Madinah, and so on), which is what makes the Calendar's time conversion work.
  • Print, email, or copy the days you select, and use the quick-select buttons to grab a whole leg of the trip at once.

Calendar

A weekly grid that pulls in everything with a time on it — itinerary items and to-dos alike — colour-coded by category.

Timezones are handled for you. Every event is anchored to its destination and shown in your device's local time. Planning from home, a 10:00 activity in Cairo shows in your home time; once you land, the same event shows in local time — no mental math, and it stays correct across the trip's different timezones.
  • Adjust the visible hours and step forward or back a week; jump to the trip start or to today.
  • Tap any event to open and edit it.

To-Do & Notes

  • To-Do: each task can hold a category, priority, status, date and time, duration, an owner, a link to an itinerary day, and a note. Filter the list, check items off, and edit in place. Anything with a time also shows up on the Calendar.
  • Notes: jot anything down, give it a category and a context (Itinerary, Expenses, and so on), and pin the ones you want at the top.

Expenses

A full spending tracker built for a multi-traveler, multi-country trip.

  • Multi-currency with live conversion — enter spending in USD, EGP, or SAR. The Saudi riyal is fixed at its 3.75 peg, and the Egyptian pound rate refreshes from the web with a ↻ Live button (with a rate you can set by hand as a fallback). Pick a non-USD currency and the USD equivalent appears right in the entry form; the table lists both the original amount and the USD value.
  • Budgets and a chart — set a budget per category and watch progress fill against a spend chart. Every category can be added, renamed (✎), or deleted (×) — including the defaults — so the list matches your actual trip. Tap any category in the chart or budget list to jump straight to a filtered report of just that category.
  • Capture who paid, who owes, the payment method, the service date, and a due date for each item.
  • Report & filter with sortable columns, so you can slice spending any way you need.
  • Import a card statement (an Amex or Citi CSV) instead of typing rows. Each charge is automatically matched to the cardholder named on the statement — so a family member's spending lands on their own card — and the matched card is shown beside every row before you confirm. If a particular export does not list a cardholder name on each row (some Amex exports leave it out), the importer flags that and asks you to pick the card yourself. Prefer to put everything on one card? Just pick it and that choice overrides the automatic match.
  • Built-in duplicate check. As soon as an import finishes, the console compares the new charges against what is already in the tracker and flags likely duplicates — showing the closest existing entry and how confident the match is — so a statement you import twice will not double-count. Strong matches are pre-ticked for one-tap removal, and you can re-open the check at any time with 🔍 Reconcile duplicates.
  • Edit a single row in place, or change many at once — tick several expenses and use Bulk edit to set a field (category, payment method, currency, dates, and so on) across all of them in one step, with blank fields left as they were. You can also tick rows and delete several together.

Card Spend & Points

  • A progress card for each card shows how much you have spent toward its welcome-bonus minimum, how much is still to go, and a “Minimum met” badge once you are over the line — so you know to stop putting spend there. Expenses paid on a card flow in automatically (from the cardholder match when you import or enter them), and you can log manual spend on top.
  • Give each minimum a deadline. Enter the date you need to hit the spend by and the card shows the days left and the dollars-per-day you would need to finish — turning amber as the date nears and red if you are behind or past due.
  • A summary across the top of the tab gives you the whole picture at a glance: total spent across all cards, how much is left to every minimum, how many welcome bonuses are earned versus still pending, the bonus points already secured versus still at stake, and a plain-language nudge for which card to put your next dollar on.
  • Points & Miles lists your balances by program, works out whether each welcome bonus has been earned yet, and projects your total once the bonus posts.

Tools that work across the app

Shared categories & colours One set of categories runs through the whole app. Edit a name or colour from To-Do, Calendar, or Itinerary and it updates everywhere — including how the Calendar is colour-coded.
Export to CSV or Excel On Expenses, To-Do, Notes, and Card Spend, tick the rows you want (or select all) and export. Column headers carry over, and the Excel file keeps proper formatting — numbers as numbers, dates as dates, amounts as currency.
Copy / duplicate Tick a row and hit Copy to clone it in place, marked “(copy)” — quick when you are entering several similar expenses or tasks.
Search everything The top search bar reaches across every tab at once and takes you straight to the match.

Share a read-only calendar

The Calendar's Share button hands someone a view-only link — through your phone's share sheet, or by email. They see the schedule in their own timezone and can look but not change anything. Because the calendar travels inside the link itself, it sends best over iMessage or email.

Add to-dos from Siri or the home screen

The console accepts to-do items via a URL, which means you can add tasks hands-free with a two-step Apple Shortcut — no need to open the console and type into the form.

Setting up the Shortcut (one time, ~2 minutes):

  1. Open the Shortcuts app on your iPhone and tap + to create a new shortcut.
  2. Add an Ask for Input action. Set the prompt to something like “Trip task?” and the input type to Text.
  3. Add an Open URL action. Set the URL to:
    https://safe2005.github.io/trip-console/?addtask=[Provided Input]
    (Tap “Provided Input” from the variable picker — Shortcuts will URL-encode it automatically.)
  4. Name the shortcut Trip Task and tap Done.

Using it:

  • Say “Hey Siri, Trip Task”, dictate the task, and it lands in the To-Do tab automatically.
  • Or tap the shortcut from the home screen, Shortcuts widget, or share sheet.
  • You can optionally add a category by appending &cat=Transport to the URL (use any category name that exists in the console).

Your data & your devices

  • Private by default. Your data lives in the browser you use it in — it is not posted anywhere automatically.
  • Sync across devices (optional). Connect your own private GitHub store from Settings to keep the same data on your phone and laptop. Skip it and everything simply stays on the one device.
  • Works offline. Keep using it with no signal; it catches up the next time you are online.
  • Travel-friendly. Low Data Mode keeps it light on patchy or pricey connections.

Good to know

  • Use a current browser (Safari, Chrome, Edge, or Firefox) for the best experience.
  • On a phone, adding it to your Home Screen makes it feel like a real app.
  • If you are looking at a review copy, the figures and names in it are placeholders for testing — not real data.
